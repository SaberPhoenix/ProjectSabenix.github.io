Only You_DUY-ANH

In many countries, names can contain many significant meanings for an individual. For instance, the protagonist’s name: “Duy-Anh,” derives from the Vietnamese culture, which means “Only You”. The character’s parents specifically named him with this name to highlight the importance to become the best, as such, the family holds very high expectation for their son. However, this leaves the protagonist with moments of stress, anger, depression, and anxiety, as he feels that he’s the only one responsible for everything. A fragment of Asian households does possess this concept of the social hierarchy, as they want their child to excel academically, have an unrealistic view of perfection and respect their power of authority. It illustrates that the family's eldest member in gender has higher authority than the youngest. Even if you were correct, you couldn't have an opinion based on this viewpoint. As a result, the sensation of being ignored and neglected can be present in someone’s daily life. It may be ideal to own a better lifestyle, but there will always be a distant connection between the protagonist and the parents in relation to their cultural backgrounds, identities, and values.

In “Help Wanted”, the main protagonist applies for a job as a waitress in an Asian restaurant. Despite the character's anxiety when approaching to the owner, her talents are demonstrated through her actions and diverts the manager’s expectations. Its narrative was inspired from my coworkers’ experience in the catering industry in my family’s business. The location will be situated at a small Vietnamese restaurant located at Saint-Michel named “Pho Duy-Anh”. Sydney Shun, a fellow colleague at Concordia will portray the main protagonist and the managers will be portrayed by both of my family members, as they’ll evaluate Sydney’s performance.
