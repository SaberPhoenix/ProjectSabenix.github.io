Artist Statement

Whether it is the physical or digital realm, my artistic view can be adapted in many forms, but filmmaking is a distinctive talent of mine. My journey into video creation began on “April 14, 2014”, with an autodidactic work ethic and the ambition to strive in social media. A major element in my art practice, would be the representation of identity, emotions, and culture. Growing up, I had many struggles establishing my own personality and felt set apart from society.

Although Canada is a multicultural community, when reuniting with others of the same nation or origin can feel alienated from a difference in social status. The effects of culture shock can be displayed in many forms, such as in languages, perspectives, and education. Filmmaking allows for a visual representation of storytelling that guides the audience to visualize my thoughts and perspectives. The visual aesthetic and content to replicate documentaries, short narrative films or animations is a way of expression that forms bonds with my audiences from the expended world of my stories. I acknowledge that the representation of the Asian community is lacking, hence the willingness to utilize my personal experiences that defy stereotypes and archetypes that have been developed in the classical age of Hollywood.

Many of my stories tend to be paralleled to my reality, reaching out to other people’s perspectives allows the engagement of my environment and illustrate the human nature within the community.
